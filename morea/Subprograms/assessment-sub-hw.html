• Many students are confused by registers, feeling that they should appear in C code. But in C we only have global variables (data segment) or local variables (stack). Changing a register value does nothing from the perspective of high-level code, until global or local variables are modified. Something like "mov EBX, [EBP+8]" doesn't modify the data segment or the stack, and therefore does "nothing". If then one does "inc EBX", that still does "nothing". But now, if I do "mov [EBP-4], EBX" THEN I am setting a local variable to 1 plus the first parameter.
